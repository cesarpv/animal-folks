WHO WE SERVE

Animals in Minnesota. We also serve Minnesota residents who believe animals should be treated humanely and authorities who are involved in the enforcement of animal law.


MISSION

The mission of Animal Folks is to protect animals and fight animal cruelty by creating a modern system of animal law enforcement in Minnesota. Animal neglect and cruelty have been happening for centuries. We don’t expect to change attitudes about animals overnight, but we’re at least going to try and provoke discussion.

OUR POSITION

Animal Folks (AF) is a 501c3 research-based animal protection organization. The purpose of our research is to lay a foundation for systemic change (i.e., processes, tools, training) involving animals in Minnesota, so to help improve enforcement of these laws. As part of our efforts, AF also files animal cruelty complaints and conducts considerable research on the pet trade. Donations are tax-deductible.

ANIMAL FOLKS TAKES LEGAL ACTIONS AGAINST USDA

Animal Folks is part of a coalition, led by the Animal Legal Defense Fund (ALDF), that filed a lawsuit against the USDA for removal of animal welfare documents from the USDA website. Learn more: Lawsuit - USDA

VETERINARY MANUAL: Reporting Animal Cruelty.

Animal Folks has created a manual to assist veterinarians in Minnesota on how to identify and report animal cruelty. This manual has been adapted for Oklahoma and is being adapted for Massachusetts. A pdf version is at: Veterinary Manual

STATE: VET IMMUNITY BILL

Animal Folks MN introduced a bill at the MN Legislature to provide civil immunity to veterinarians for good faith reporting of animal cruelty. Learn more: Vet Immunity Bill

TRAINING: ANIMAL CRUELTY REPORTING - Veterinarians

On February 14, 2018, Animal Folks held training at the MVMA Annual Conference for veterinarians on their role in animal cruelty investigations. Learn more: Veterinary Training.
